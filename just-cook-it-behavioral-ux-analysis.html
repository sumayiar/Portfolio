Just Cook It

Behavioral UX Analysis

Case Study Snapshot for Recruiters & Hiring Managers

In Just Cook It, I am implementing BJ Fogg's Behavior Model to help users build sustainable cooking habits through motivation, ability, and timely prompts.

My goal was to "help people do what they already want to do and help people feel successful." To support that goal, I analyzed human decision making and used those insights to guide the product's behavioral design choices.

I also acknowledged the cultural context and tailored the design accordingly for busy Americans.

This work reflects how I combine UX research, experience design, and frontend development to translate behavioral insights into practical product decisions and implementation-ready flows.

Additional Behavioral Framework: CREATE Action Funnel

Behavioral Strategy Layer for Increased Cooking Frequency

In addition to BJ Fogg's Behavior Model, I implemented Stephen Wendel's CREATE Action Funnel to make this behavior strategy explicit and actionable.

I chose to explore the CREATE Action Funnel because it motivates action through six key stages, helping identify where users drop off and what design interventions can move them forward.

  • Prioritized user motivation and confidence across each stage of action
  • Mapped friction points to specific UX and content decisions
  • Designed prompts and flows that encourage users to cook more consistently
